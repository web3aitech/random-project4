Glass Reinforced Pipes

Definition: Glass Reinforced Plastic (GRP) pipes are made from glass-fiber reinforcements embedded in cured thermosetting resin; they may contain granular aggregate. The selection of proper material and composition depends on the required properties and designed performance characteristics.

GRP pipes were introduced in 1948. Since then GRP invaded the market due to its corrosion-resistance capabilities and was selected as an alternative to steel and stainless steel. The GRP pipe range expanded to include high-pressure applications and a big range of diameters reaching up to 4000 mm.

GRP pipes combine the benefits of durability, strength, and corrosion resistance; they also offer great design flexibility - properties, fittings profiles and shapes can be customized across a wide range.

Materials

GRP pipes consist of glass-fiber reinforcement (the mechanical strength depends on the amount, type and arrangement of glass) and thermosetting resin, cured by heat and/or chemical additives.

Common glass types used in GRP pipes:

DesignationGeneral application
EGood electrical resistance
ECR or AdvantexGood acid resistance
CGood chemical resistance

Fiberglass reinforcements are available in different forms:

  • Continuous roving - bundled, untwisted strand; provides excellent mechanical properties.
  • Reinforcing mat - chopped strands held together with binder; multidirectional reinforcement in pipes and fittings.
  • Surface veil - lightweight, allows high-resin-content layers; improves environmental resistance and gives a smooth surface.

Only thermosetting resins are used in GRP pipe production:

  • Polyester (e.g. isophthalic) - commonly used for large-diameter water and sewage pipes. Vinyl ester is another polyester with increased corrosion resistance, used for aggressive fluids such as acids.
  • Epoxy - commonly used for small-diameter pipes conveying water, hydrocarbons and diluted acids.

Additional components may include silica sand, organic-peroxide catalysts, hardeners and accelerators.

Production Process

GRP pipes are produced by Dubai Pipes Factory by either Continuous Filament Winding or Reciprocal Helical Winding. Both are filament-winding processes: impregnating glass reinforcement with resin, then applying the wet fibers onto a mandrel in a prescribed pattern.

Continuous Filament Winder

A continuous winder is composed of a continuous steel band supported by beams which form a cylindrically shaped mandrel. The beams rotate, friction pulls the band around, and roller bearings allow the band to move longitudinally so that the entire mandrel continuously moves in a spiral path toward the end of the machine. As the mandrel moves, raw materials - glass fibers, resin, aggregate (if needed) and surface veil - are metered on in precise amounts under the direction of a programmable logic controller (PLC) and computer. After the pipe is cured, a synchronized saw unit cuts it to the proper length.

Continuous winding is the most efficient process: once production starts it continues without holding and resuming at each pipe or layer, stopping only at the completion of the full order.

Reciprocal Helical Winding

Glass roving (continuous roving specifically) passes through a resin bath for impregnation. The resin bath and glass-directing comb are placed on a carriage driven back and forth at a well-controlled linear speed. Fiberglass, pre-impregnated with resin, is wound onto a steel mandrel rotating at a determined RPM. The synchronization of carriage linear speed and mandrel RPM determines the angle at which roving is wound - the glass winding angle, predetermined by the requested pipe property. Both mandrel RPM and carriage speed are computer-controlled.

Winding progresses in stages: multiple linear movements of the carriage produce one layer of pipe thickness, and the total thickness is composed of multiple layers depending on the required thickness.

Pipe layers

For all processes, GRP pipes are made from many layers. The main layers are:

  • Inner liner - resin-rich, provides chemical resistance and a smooth inner surface; thickness can reach up to 2.5 mm.
  • Structural layer - forms the majority of pipe thickness; mechanical strength is mainly achieved here.
  • Outer liner - resin-rich, gives environmental protection.

Fittings

All types of GRP fittings are produced by Dubai Pipes factory. Fittings are made from sections of pipe that are cut, modified, assembled to the required shape and then jointed together with hand lay-up lamination. The material used for lamination is almost identical to the material used in pipe production, and lamination properties normally exceed the pipe properties.

GRP fittings include: elbows with any bending angle; tees with equal and unequal branch; Y with different branch angle and branch diameter; reducers (concentric and eccentric); and nozzles.

Fittings are produced with a variable range of ends:

  • Spigot end - to suit GRP standard Reka coupler
  • Modified spigot end - to match other GRP and non-GRP piping
  • Flanged ends - drilled to match any GRP or non-GRP flange
  • Plain end - for further jointing by lamination

Manhole Liners

Manhole liners are made in whatever shape required. A thin pipe produced at the winder forms the manhole shaft; the cover slab with the required opening is sprayed on a pre-formed mold. Sprayed cover slabs use glass fiber and resin identical to those used for pipe production. The cover slab can be jointed to the shaft either in-plant or on-site by lamination or by bell-mouth coupling.
